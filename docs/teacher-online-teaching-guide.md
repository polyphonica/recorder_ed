## Critical: Audio Settings for Music Teaching

### Why Audio Settings Matter
Standard video conferencing platforms are designed for speech, not music. They automatically:
- ‚ùå Suppress background noise (including musical instruments!)
- ‚ùå Cancel echoes (removing resonance and harmonics)
- ‚ùå Compress audio (losing musical detail)

**You MUST disable these features for music teaching.**

---

## Zoom: Enabling "Original Sound"

### Initial Setup (One-Time)
1. Open Zoom desktop app
2. Click your profile picture ‚Üí **Settings**
3. Go to **Audio** tab
4. Check **"Show in-meeting option to 'Enable Original Sound' from client"**
5. **IMPORTANT:** Also check these settings:
   - ‚úÖ Suppress background noise: **Low** (or Off)
   - ‚úÖ Echo cancellation: **Auto** or **Aggressive** (keep this on to avoid feedback)

### During Each Lesson
1. Start or join the meeting
2. Click the arrow next to **Mute** button (bottom left)
3. Click **"Turn on Original Sound"**
4. You'll see "Original Sound: On" at the top left

**Pro Tip:** Original Sound must be enabled for EACH meeting - it doesn't stay on by default.

### Testing Your Audio
Before your first lesson:
1. Ask a musical friend to join a test meeting
2. Play your instrument
3. Ask them to confirm they can hear the full, natural sound
4. Check that fast passages aren't cutting out

## Google Meet: Disabling Noise Cancellation

### Free Account (Limited Options)
Unfortunately, free Google accounts have limited noise cancellation controls.

**During Meeting:**
1. Join meeting
2. Click the three dots (‚ãÆ) ‚Üí **Settings**
3. Go to **Audio** tab
4. If available, turn **Noise cancellation** to **Off**

**Note:** This option may not be available on free accounts.

### Workspace Account (Full Control)

**Desktop:**
1. Join meeting
2. Click three dots ‚Üí **Settings**
3. Go to **Audio** tab
4. Set **Noise cancellation** to **Off**

**Mobile:**
1. Join meeting and tap screen
2. Tap three dots ‚Üí **Settings**
3. Go to **Audio**
4. Toggle **Noise cancellation** off

### Important Note
Audio from screen sharing (like playing a YouTube video) is NOT affected by noise cancellation - students can hear it properly.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "Student can't hear my instrument properly"
**Fix:**
1. Ensure "Original Sound" (Zoom) is enabled
2. Check noise cancellation is OFF (Google Meet/Teams)
3. Speak closer to microphone, then play instrument at same distance
4. Consider upgrading to external USB microphone

### "Audio is cutting out or choppy"
**Fix:**
1. Check internet speed (need 10+ Mbps)
2. Switch to wired ethernet if using WiFi
3. Close other applications using internet
4. Ask others in house to pause streaming/downloads
5. Reduce video quality if needed (Zoom: Video Settings ‚Üí HD unchecked)

### "Student hears an echo"
**Fix:**
1. Ask student to use headphones
2. You should also use headphones
3. Check that echo cancellation is enabled in your audio settings

### "Video freezes but audio works"
**Fix:**
1. Turn off your video temporarily (audio continues)
2. Screen share notation/materials instead
3. Check internet bandwidth

### "Can't enable Original Sound in Zoom"
**Fix:**
1. Must use Zoom desktop app (not web browser)
2. Check Settings ‚Üí Audio ‚Üí "Show in-meeting option..." is checked
3. Restart Zoom application

## Recommendations by Teaching Style

### Teaching Mostly 1-on-1 Lessons
**Best Choice: Zoom Free or Google Meet Free**
- Both offer unlimited 1-on-1 time
- Zero cost
- Zoom has better audio controls
- Google Meet has 20 extra minutes for occasional group sessions

### Teaching Regular Group Workshops
**Best Choice: Zoom Pro (¬£11.99/month)**
- Unlimited duration
- Best audio for music
- Most familiar to students
- Professional features (waiting room, breakout rooms)

### Already Using Microsoft 365
**Best Choice: Microsoft Teams**
- Included with your existing subscription
- Good integration with OneDrive for materials
- Adequate audio settings for music

### Budget-Conscious
**Best Choice: Zoom Free**
- Most of your lessons will be 1-on-1 (unlimited)
- Occasional group workshops can work in 40-minute blocks
- Best audio quality for free tier
- Upgrade to Pro later if needed
